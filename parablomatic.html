What are Quadratics?

Quadratic equations are second degree polynomials, or in other words, they are equations in which at least one term is raised to the power of two(and there are no other terms raised to higher powers).

Quadratic equations form a special shape when graphed, a parabola.

A parabola first goes down(or up), and after a certain point it goes back up (or down). They also have an axis of symmetry, the axis of symmetry cuts the parabola in half, and produces two mirror images.
